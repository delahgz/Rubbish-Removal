What Can and Cannot Be Recycled in Sydney

Sydney's recycling system is simpler than most people think, but there are some common mistakes that contaminate entire loads. This guide explains exactly what goes in your yellow lid bin, what needs special disposal, and how to handle items that cannot be recycled at home.

The yellow bin basics

Your yellow lid recycling bin is for clean, dry recyclables. The key word is "clean" - contaminated items can ruin entire truckloads of otherwise recyclable material.

Yes - Recycle these

  • Paper and cardboard (flattened)
  • Glass bottles and jars
  • Aluminium and steel cans
  • Hard plastic containers (#1-5)
  • Milk and juice cartons
  • Aerosol cans (empty)

No - Do not recycle

  • Soft plastics (bags, wrap)
  • Polystyrene/styrofoam
  • Food waste
  • Nappies
  • Clothing and textiles
  • Electronics
  • Batteries
The scrunch test: If you can scrunch a plastic item into a ball and it stays scrunched, it is soft plastic and should not go in the yellow bin. Examples: chip packets, bread bags, cling wrap.

Common recycling mistakes

Putting items in plastic bags

Never put recyclables inside plastic bags. Items need to be loose in the bin. Bagged recyclables often go straight to landfill because sorting facilities cannot open every bag.

Food contamination

Containers do not need to be spotless, but they should be empty and reasonably clean. A quick rinse is enough. A pizza box with grease stains? Tear off the clean lid for recycling and bin the greasy base.

Wishful recycling

Putting items in recycling because you hope they can be recycled is called "wish-cycling." If you are not sure, it is better to put it in general waste than contaminate recyclables.

Items that need special disposal

E-waste

Electronics, including phones, computers, TVs, and appliances, cannot go in any household bin. Most councils have e-waste drop-off points, or you can take them to Officeworks or similar retailers.

Batteries

All batteries need special disposal. Aldi, Officeworks, and many council facilities have battery recycling bins. Never put batteries in general waste as they can cause fires.

Paint and chemicals

Household hazardous waste including paint, pesticides, pool chemicals, and cleaning products needs to go to a community recycling centre. Check your council website for locations.

Mattresses

Mattresses cannot go in council cleanup or regular waste. They need professional removal. We take mattresses and ensure they go to proper recycling facilities where possible.

Soft plastics

Since the REDcycle program ended in 2022, soft plastic recycling options in Sydney are limited. Some councils are trialling kerbside soft plastic collection. Check your local council for current options.
